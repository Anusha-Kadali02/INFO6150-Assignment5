THINGS TO DO IN BOSTON

Welcome to Boston

Boston invites visitors to explore America's past and vibrant culture. Trace the Freedom Trail through the heart of the city, where colorful neighborhoods are dotted with historic sites and parks. Try the chowder at Boston's oldest restaurant, head to the Italian-accented North End for cannoli and pizza, or snack on hot dogs in the stands at Fenway Park. Browse collections of masterpiece artwork in the city's many museums, then get beyond the city limits on a day trip to old-fashioned villages, Cape Cod's sandy beaches, or one of New England's top shopping destinations.

Boston Common

20,908

Boston Common is one of the oldest public parks in the United States, dating back to 1634. It is a historic and iconic green space located in the heart of Boston, Massachusetts, serving as a central gathering place for both residents and visitors.

Massachusetts State House

19,976

The Massachusetts State House, located in Boston, is a historic and iconic building that serves as the seat of the state government. Designed by Charles Bulfinch and completed in 1798, it is known for its distinctive golden dome, making it a prominent symbol of Massachusetts politics and history.

Faneuil Hall Marketplace

12,480

Faneuil Hall Marketplace is a historic marketplace located in Boston, Massachusetts. It is a vibrant gathering place known for its diverse shopping, dining, and entertainment options, and it has a rich history dating back to the 18th century.

Boston North End

14,137

The North End is a historic neighborhood in Boston, Massachusetts, known for its charming streets, Italian heritage, and delectable cuisine. It's often called "Little Italy" and is famous for its authentic Italian restaurants and the iconic Old North Church.

Boston Public Library

34,569

The Boston Public Library, founded in 1848, is one of the oldest and most iconic public library systems in the United States. It serves as a cultural and educational hub, housing an extensive collection of books, manuscripts, and art, while providing free access to knowledge and resources for the people of Boston and beyond.

Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum

22,194

The Boston Tea Party Ships & Museum is a historical attraction in Boston, Massachusetts, dedicated to preserving and commemorating the famous 1773 Boston Tea Party protest. Visitors can explore authentically restored tea ships and participate in interactive exhibits, immersing themselves in the events that shaped American history.
